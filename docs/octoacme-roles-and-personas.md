# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-friendly interfaces and experiences. They conduct research, design prototypes, and validate that solutions meet user needs through testing and iteration.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers on feature design
- Ensure accessibility and consistency across the product
- Advocate for the user in design and prioritization decisions

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across features and releases

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques
- Prototypes and design specs shared with engineering
- User research findings and recommendations
- Weekly syncs with Product and Engineering

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain documentation that helps users and developers understand and use the product effectively. They ensure documentation is clear, accurate, and up-to-date.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API documentation, and release notes
- Collaborate with developers to understand features and changes
- Review documentation for clarity, accuracy, and consistency
- Organize and structure documentation for easy navigation
- Ensure documentation is updated with each release

### Goals
- Provide clear, comprehensive documentation for all users
- Reduce support requests through better documentation
- Maintain documentation quality and consistency

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with engineering and product teams
- Release notes and user-facing documentation
- Feedback sessions with support and customer-facing teams
- Updates and announcements about documentation changes

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, funding approval, and business context for projects. They ensure alignment with organizational goals and make key decisions on scope, priority, and resourcing.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria
- Approve project scope, budget, and timeline
- Provide strategic guidance and remove organizational barriers
- Review progress and make go/no-go decisions at key milestones
- Champion the project within the broader organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and align with strategy
- Allocate resources effectively across initiatives
- Enable teams to succeed by removing obstacles
- Maintain visibility into project health and outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and milestone reviews
- Escalation point for critical decisions and risks
- Project charter approval and scope changes
- Strategic planning and roadmap discussions

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure that projects follow security best practices, identify and mitigate security risks, and maintain compliance with security policies. They are the subject matter experts for security concerns.

### Responsibilities
- Review designs and code for security vulnerabilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria
- Conduct threat modeling and security assessments
- Respond to security incidents and coordinate remediation
- Ensure compliance with security policies and standards
- Provide security training and guidance to the team

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Maintain a strong security posture across all projects
- Ensure rapid response and remediation of security issues
- Build security awareness and capability within teams

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during design and code review phases
- Threat assessments and security requirements
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Security bulletins and vulnerability advisories
- Guidance on security tools and best practices
